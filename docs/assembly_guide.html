Overview

What this build includes

The Open NDVI Handheld uses a modular assembly approach. A custom carrier PCB hosts the ESP32 Feather V2, two AS7265x Triad sensors, an ST7789 LCD, the measurement button, power switch, and the battery connection.

View wiring and pinout Read troubleshooting FAQ

Parts

Required components

  • Adafruit ESP32 Feather V2
  • 2x SparkFun AS7265x Triad spectral sensors
  • Pimoroni 1.3" 240x240 SPI LCD with ST7789 controller
  • Single-cell LiPo battery
  • Custom carrier PCB
  • Power switch and momentary measurement button
  • Headers, connectors, and 3D printed enclosure parts

Preparation

Before assembly

  • Fabricate the carrier PCB from the Gerbers in `hardware/gerbers/`.
  • Review `hardware/outputs/pcb_top.pdf` and `pcb_bottom.pdf`.
  • Print the enclosure parts from `mechanical/` if you want a full housing.
  • Confirm the firmware target is the ESP32 Feather V2 project in `firmware/src/`.

Sequence

Recommended assembly order

  1. Install headers and board-to-board connectors on the carrier PCB.
  2. Mount the ESP32 Feather V2 to the carrier board.
  3. Mount and connect the upward-facing AS7265x sensor for incident light measurement.
  4. Mount and connect the downward-facing AS7265x sensor for reflected light measurement.
  5. Connect the ST7789 display over SPI.
  6. Wire the power switch and the measurement button.
  7. Connect the LiPo battery and verify battery monitor routing.
  8. Test power, display, both I2C buses, and the button before closing the enclosure.
  9. Install the assembled electronics into the printed enclosure.

Checks

Bring-up checklist

  • ESP32 powers and enumerates over USB.
  • Display backlight turns on.
  • Both sensors initialise without bus collisions.
  • Battery percentage appears on-screen.
  • Measurement button triggers a reading.

Notes

Assembly intent

  • The project is modular by design, so component replacement is straightforward.
  • The dual-sensor layout is central to the incident/reflected light workflow.
  • Use the wiring page for the actual pin mapping before soldering permanent connections.

Next

After physical assembly

Once the hardware is assembled, program the Feather with the PlatformIO project in `firmware/`, then verify the display, sensor readings, battery sampling, and BLE status behaviour.
