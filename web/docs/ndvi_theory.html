Measurement Principles

NDVI Theory

NDVI is not measured directly. It is computed from red and near-infrared reflectance behaviour, with this device using a dual-sensor design to improve interpretation under changing outdoor light.

Definition

What NDVI is

NDVI relates plant condition to how vegetation interacts with light. Healthy vegetation tends to absorb red light for photosynthesis while reflecting more near-infrared light from internal leaf structure. 

NDVI = (NIR - RED) / (NIR + RED)

Interpretation

Typical value ranges

  • 0.5 to 0.9: dense, healthy vegetation
  • 0.2 to 0.5: sparse, stressed, or partially senescent vegetation
  • 0.0 to 0.2: very sparse vegetation or mixed soil/background
  • Below 0.0: soil, concrete, water, or other non-vegetated surfaces

Measurement model

What this device actually measures

The device measures spectral intensity in discrete wavelength bands using photodiode-based spectral sensors. NDVI is then derived from selected bands representing red and near-infrared response.

  • Measurements are relative reflected light intensities.
  • The system is not claiming absolute radiometric calibration.
  • The firmware documents the exact band selection used for NDVI calculation.

Why two sensors

Incident and reflected light

Outdoor illumination is unstable. Cloud cover, solar angle, shadows, and atmosphere all change the available light. A single downward-looking sensor would mix vegetation response with changing illumination.

  • UP sensor: measures incident light illuminating the scene
  • DOWN sensor: measures reflected light from the target surface

Benefit

Why the ratio matters

Measuring both incident and reflected light improves repeatability and short-term comparability by normalising the vegetation signal against the illumination conditions at the moment of capture.

Limits

Assumptions and constraints

  • Illumination should be reasonably uniform across the field of view.
  • The measured area should mostly contain the intended surface.
  • Strong shadows, specular reflections, and mixed backgrounds reduce reliability.
  • The device is a transparent reference build, not a certified agronomic instrument.
The purpose of this project is to expose the measurement chain, not to hide it behind a single unexplained number.
